Plant Progress: Viola (Johnny Jump Up)

Day 1 - April 8, 2018

The first seeds have been planted in both the independent and dependent variables. I was unsure how to correctly plant the seeds but I just placed several in the pots. I hope that initial watering of the plants were enough as I did develop a makeshift watering system using a water pipe will holes along side it. I do have doubts about how well the water pipes were placed and the automation of the watering system. Hopefully everything goes well and something does actually grow.

Posted on April 8, 2018 by Lexzander Saplan

Day 6 - April 14, 2018

Some of the seeds in the pot with automatic watering have sprouted! However, there was still no change in the not automatically watered pot. The sprouts can be seen at the bottom left of the pot. During my research, it was stated that Viola germination would take 10-15 days so I was actually surprised to see some progress already so early on. I hope that nothing will happen to these seedlinds so I can show some greater progress when they become flowers.

Posted on April 14, 2018 by Lexzander Saplan

Day 8 - April 16, 2018

Many of the seeds have begun sprouting tall stalks with two leaves in just two days after the previous update! The other pot has yet to see any seedslings yet and I am curious to know why. I have begun to switch the lighting for the automized plant with blue light as this will efectively help grow the plants in this stage. The water moisture sensor got corroded and I had to switch it out for another one as it was no longer taking any accurate measurements. If there was progress within a week of planting, I would like to see the progress a week from today!

Posted on April 16 2018 by Lexzander Saplan
